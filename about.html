About 406 Technical Services

30+ years of engineering experience, now applied locally.

Steve Akers founded 406 Technical Services in 2024 after three decades as a hands-on network and systems engineer at Nextel, Sprint, Clearwire, and Lumen Technologies. 406TS exists to bring that background to bear on a different kind of problem: helping Montana businesses and households make smart technology decisions without getting sold something they don't need.

At Lumen, Steve was a Tier 4 Network SME — the final escalation point for 30+ datacenters across the US, Canada, Europe, and Asia — responsible for Juniper MX/SRX/QFX, Cisco N9000, and Arista infrastructure. He later was a key contributor to the NOC's migration from manual monitoring to automated ticket creation, eliminating hundreds of hours of repetitive work each month. Before that, at Samsung SDS, he built the IP core infrastructure for Sprint's LTE network expansion. At Nextel in the early 2000s, he was the technical lead for E911 — and was nominated for the company's Circle of Excellence for identifying and resolving a GPS defect that affected emergency call accuracy across the network.

That history means you're not hiring a generalist who learned networking from YouTube. You're working with someone who has diagnosed carrier-scale incidents, built automation tools from scratch, and operated production systems that millions of people depended on.

What 406TS Does

Work comes in at two scales. For local clients, Steve does hands-on work: Wi-Fi surveys and mesh deployments, Starlink installations, PC and Mac support, Microsoft 365 administration, low-voltage cabling, and camera systems. For businesses with bigger technology decisions — connectivity, cloud, UCaaS/CCaaS, cybersecurity, AI — Steve acts as a vendor-neutral advisor and brokers multi-vendor quotes through OTG Consulting, which is free to the client.

OTG Consulting Partnership

As an OTG Consulting channel partner, 406TS has access to 220+ vetted providers and a team of 300+ engineers across every IT discipline. When a client's project is bigger than one person, Steve manages the relationship and makes sure the solution actually fits the client's situation — not just whatever earns the biggest commission.
